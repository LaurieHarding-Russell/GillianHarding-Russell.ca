Gillian Harding-Russell

gillian harding russell was born in Toronto and raised in St Jean-sur- Richelieu, Quebec. She attended McGill University where she earned her MA on the 19th century novel, and moved to Saskatchewan where she received her Ph.D in English at the University of Saskatchewan. Her dissertation on the mythopoeic element in postmodern Canadian poetry focused on the work of Gwendolyn MacEwen and Michael Ondaatje. She later lived in Surrey, B.C. where she became poetry editor at Douglas College’s Event magazine (1989- 2005), and continues as poetry editor for the Event Reading Service. Gillian has also been a sessional at both the University of Saskatchewan and the University of Regina. She has reviewed books for a number of journals over the years including (among others) Prairie Fire, FreeFall, The Queen’s Quarterly, The Goose: A Journal for the Arts, Culture and Environment and most recently for The Pacific Rim Review of Books.

She has had poems published in seventeen anthologies across Canada, and has five poetry collections and seven chapbooks (Megrim one pending) published, the most recently released Uninterrupted (2020). Her previous collection In Another Air (Radiant Press, 2018) was shortlisted for the City of Regina Book Award. Her work has been shortlisted three times for Exile’s Gwendolyn MacEwen chapbook award, and in 2016, the sequence “Making Sense” was chosen as “Best suite” by a poet at any time in her career. Her poems have also been shortlisted for the Thomas Morton award, have twice placed second in the GritLIT and twice won “Best Regina Poem” in the Briarpatch’s Writing in the Margins’ competition.
