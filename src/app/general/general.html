Here is a brief description of Domestic Disorder:
‘Disorder’ most often sets off the plot in these domestic poems. Inside the microcosm of the speaker’s
consciousness, she mulls haps and mishaps, loves and their worries, undercurrent dreams, an epileptic
dog, god-thoughts that spark thunderous feelings amid realizations that lightning can strike to light up the
dome of that skull-house. Far from tame, these household poems aspire – often jauntily, with shades of
comedy and irony – to the roots and outer vibes of the universe. I hope these poems in Domestic
Disorder will remind some readers of their own domestic experiences with house repairs, pets, accidents
and the problems that sometimes surface amid dreams, throwback memories, and out-of-this-world
wonderings that happen between.
Here is a sample poem from Domestic Disorder. Our black lab has epilepsy and this poem was written after a seizure:

There is a smell after terror


      When the dog woke in the black
      of night with a jolt, skittered
      on curled tense toenails across
      the room and back, dropped
      to the floor – lightning struck
      by what meteor of dream
      mind-neurons as stars
      seized in a personal apocalypse
      of frothing jaw, iron-clamped
      over colliding feet that covered
      galaxies on his Sirius side
      and there was a rankness,
      a bitter scent, poignant distinct
      after the electric and shock
      even as saliva and slime
      permeated my hands and hair
      we laved the exhausted dog
      with soothing wet flannel, his
      great dog heart still punching
      to escape his body, an alien
      elixir lifting into the air
      in a chemical language
      I had just one letter for
      from an alphabet
      of my own megrim
      with their electrifying fireworks,
      instant dread and high alarm,
      though I could not yet comprehend
      what word in what language
      that single shared character
      might form

      published on LCP Poetry//Pause
Two poems from a poetry manuscript-in-progress, Pareidolia :

Shades of twilight, St. Lucia’s Day

      A slow leak of sadness he felt at the end of summer with
      the fall’s first frost he thought to water the evergreens
      for winter, but called away to answer a plea from a previous
      thought he hurriedly turned off the hose with a weak twist
      of the knob and talked to a loved one a lifetime away

      the sudden worry he might not have shut off the hose
      grey months later in November a lake of tears appeared
      at the foot of the lawn bordering his neighbour’s, and then
      he thought, huh, a broken water main and would have gone
      over to warn his neighbour – he’d suffered his own
      the previous winter, but then the city-worker in neon vest
      tromped in front of the house, so he thought the matter

      dealt with when in the middle of the night a call in the deep
      of sleep woke him between dream frames reminding him
      of what he might have forgotten – did he leave the hose on?
      Might it be all the while leaking sadness over Chris’s lawn,
      a white expanse when he peeped under the curtain flap

      at first twilight on St. Lucia’s day, and later that morning
      before him an agony of glassy branches in a frozen dance
      sparkled in sunlight, an artwork of sun’s play on grief so
      that for a moment he no longer mourned the absent phone
      call from a previous life with the present surreal before him:

      the water-tap beside the house iced-over at the nozzle, no
      turning it off possible, the leak trickling at a pencil’s width
      in an underground stream as he came back inside, wandered
      down steep stairs in open back slippers and stood up on a chair
      to turn off the faucet, knocking his head on the overhead shelf

      Later in the mirror, a blood smear over one eye, he dabbed
      his forehead, huh! A cyclops, which expressed the past’s
      quiet pain, and he felt, ah well.

When I look, I see a face


      that house’s twin windows around a door
      are golden eyes either side a long muzzle
      and I see a bucolic face with round kind
      eyeballs in that 50’s Buick, but Mazda’s
      headlights slanted underwater are sharks’

      in dangerous waters with a grill-toothed
      mouth to suck in dirt and mud and
      seasonal speed-flung bug, the fender
      a visage with cruel nether lip to protect you
      from the other fool’s mountain of metal

      ‘So why do you look for faces,’ you ask
      me, ‘such kindergarten stuff!’ and I
      reply, I can’t help myself! I see eyes
      and a mouth and a nose in my teacup’s
      loose leaves and a bulge of beige nose
      in Sunday’s pancake with lopsided batter
      
      I see archangels’ faces with trumpets
      in low-floating cumulus and sometimes
      think I might see a mightier visage
      in cumulonimbus (I confess… though
      you will flatly denounce, refuse to
      countenance it) around or above, or
      is it inside my seeing? and that
      that makes me           
                                  well, what (am I?)
